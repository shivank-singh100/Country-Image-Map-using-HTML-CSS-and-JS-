Places to visit in Farrukhabad

Mathiya Devi Temple

The Mathiya Devi Temple is one of the most revered temples in Farrukhabad. It is dedicated to the goddess Mathiya Devi, and the temple is a major pilgrimage site.
To reach here: It’s 3.5 km from the railway station.

Sankisa Temple

Following the death of Gautam Buddha, the great king Ashoka developed this region. He constructed the enormous Ashoka pillar in this town, which still stands today and is known as Elephant Capital.
Many travelers from countries other than India frequented this place all year round.
The place is well known for having ties to past Buddhists and Buddhas. Buddhists hold that the Buddha returned to Earth after spending three months in the heavens.
To reach here: It’s 3.5 km from the railway station.

Shri Pandveshwar Nath Dham


It’s popularly known as Panda Bagh Mandir. Folklore claims that the Pandavas constructed it while they were exiled.
One of Lord Shiva’s oldest temples. The Shivling is thought to have been created by the Pandavas while they were on Agyatvas.
Temples for Lord Hanuman and other deities can be seen along the temple corridor. On Mahashivratri and Sawan, a large number of worshippers visit this place.
To reach here: It’s 800 meters from the railway station and can be covered on foot.

Gurgaon Devi Temple

The Devi temple is well known for being self-manifested (Swayambhu). It is believed that Dronacharya’s Guru once stood in this location.
There is a place called Mace of Bheem, which is located close to this temple. It is an iron mace that does not catch rust and is reasoned to be of Bhimsen of the Mahabharata.
To reach here: Temple is located at a distance of 3.8 km from the railway station.

Panchal Ghat

This ghat is connected to the holy Ganges. This ghat has Panchali’s (Draupadi’s) name. To wash away their crimes, they bathe in the Ganga. Lots of tribes live here by making their tents. It’s also a good place to enjoy shopping, as it includes a series of shops with lots of variety.
Every year, The Kite contest is one of the city’s greatest attractions.
To reach here: It’s almost 7.6 km from Farrukhabad railway station. Public vehicles are easily available from both places, directly to the ghat.

Neeb Karori

Neeb Karori is a tiny village close to antique Sankisha that is well-known for the sage Lakshman Das.
He is commonly referred to by the name Baba Neeb Karori, and he built numerous Lord Hanuman temples around India, including one in Farrukhabad.
To reach here: It’s at a distance of 26 km from the railway station and 11 km from the airport. Any means of transport is easily available.
